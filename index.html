About

I work at the interface of remote sensing, soil science, and machine learning. The goal is to turn rich spectral and structural information into actionable estimates of subsurface carbon and ecosystem function.

My background spans both academic research and practical geospatial analysis. I hold a Masters in Geographic Information Science and I am currently an ABD PhD candidate in Geoinformatics and Geospatial Analytics at Saint Louis University, where I serve as a Graduate Assistant in the Remote Sensing Lab.

My work focuses on depth resolved soil organic carbon mapping in sorghum and other cropping systems using UAV based VNIR SWIR hyperspectral imagery, LiDAR, and multi scale field measurements.

Methodologically, I am interested in physics aware neural networks, texture based features, and spectral transformations that respect soil physics and radiative transfer.
