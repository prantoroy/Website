About

Remote sensing, soil science, and machine learning — turned into actionable subsurface intelligence.

I hold a Master's in Geographic Information Science and am currently an ABD PhD candidate in Geoinformatics and Geospatial Analytics at Saint Louis University, where I serve as a Graduate Research Assistant in the Remote Sensing Lab.

My work focuses on depth-resolved soil organic carbon mapping using hyperspectral imagery and multi-scale field measurements across multi-year field campaigns in sorghum and other cropping systems.

Methodologically I am interested in physics-aware neural networks, wavelet-based spectral transforms, and foundation model embeddings that respect soil physics and radiative transfer to push inference below what surface optics can directly sense.

Research interests

Hyperspectral imaging of soil and vegetation Depth-resolved subsurface carbon mapping Physics-informed and physics-aware deep learning Multimodal sensor fusion Foundation models for Earth observation Spatio-temporal modeling of agroecosystems Proactive crop stress forecasting Open and reproducible geospatial workflows
